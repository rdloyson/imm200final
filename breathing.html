Breathing

Mindful breathing is a very basic yet powerful mindfulness meditation practice. The idea is simply to focus your attention on your breathing to its natural rhythm and flow and the way it feels on each inhale and exhale.

Deep breathing is a form of meditation, a practice that researchers say dates back several thousand years. Research shows that meditation can reduce anxiety, sharpen memory, treat symptoms of depression, promote more restful sleep, and even improve heart health. There are thousands of forms of meditation and with them come different breathing techniques.

Yogis have used pranayama techniques for centuries to regulate the breath and prepare the mind for deep meditation. Even in modern forms of yoga, the breath is a vital part of the practice, allowing the body to flow effortlessly through asanas, or yoga poses. The key to deep meditation is calming the mind and body before your practice, and breathing exercises can do just that. There are techniques such as the 4-7-8 technique, that you breathe in quietly for 4 seconds, keep in your breath for 7 seconds, then you exhale for 8 seconds.
